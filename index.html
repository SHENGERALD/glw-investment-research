Glass Carrier — TSMC CoPoS

TSMC's Chip-on-Panel-on-Substrate (CoPoS) uses 310×310mm square glass carriers for next-gen AI packaging. Corning is the established supplier of fusion-drawn glass carriers — the only company producing panels at semiconductor-grade quality at scale.

TSMC pilot 2026 Mass production 2028–29 NVIDIA first adopter 90% trial yields

Glass Core Substrate (GCS) — Intel, AMD/Absolics

Replaces ABF core layer with glass, enabling 10x interconnect density. Intel's NEPCON Japan 2026 demo showed first thick-core (10-2-10) glass substrate with EMIB — zero SeWaRe micro-cracks. AMD certifying with Absolics, targeting MI400-series AI accelerators.

Intel HVM H2 2026 AMD certification Q1-Q2 2026 600+ Intel patents <20μm warpage

Glass Interposer — Samsung

Samsung Electronics confirmed 2028 introduction of glass interposers replacing silicon interposers for GPU/HBM connections. Samsung SEMCO pilot production active at Sejong — project elevated from R&D to Business Unit in Feb 2026. This is the major catalyst.

Samsung SEMCO mass prod. 2027 Samsung Electronics adoption 2028 Samples to AMD, Broadcom ₩12T market by 2028
Key Insight — Source of Alpha: NVIDIA Vera Rubin (2026) does NOT use glass substrates. This is the market's misunderstanding. But Glass Carriers are already in TSMC CoPoS today, AMD is certifying glass core substrates, and Samsung's 2028 Glass Interposer plan is the real major catalyst. The opportunity doesn't depend on NVIDIA.
